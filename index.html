About This Course

This course blends biology, chemistry, physics, and critical thinking through crime scene analysis, evidence testing, and case-based investigations.

👁️

Observation First

We start by studying perception, attention, bias, and memory so students understand why evidence matters.

🧪

Science in Action

Students apply real scientific reasoning to fingerprints, blood, DNA, trace evidence, toxicology, and more.

📁

Case-Based Learning

Each unit connects to investigations, suspect statements, physical evidence, and courtroom-style conclusions.

🧭

Thinking Like an Investigator

Students learn to slow down, document carefully, challenge assumptions, and defend conclusions with evidence.

How to Succeed in This Class

Strong forensic students are careful, curious, and evidence-driven. This course rewards good thinking more than fast guessing.

✅
Write everything down.
Good notes beat shaky memory.
✅
Separate observation from inference.
Describe what is there before deciding what it means.
✅
Challenge your assumptions.
Bias is one of the biggest threats to accurate conclusions.
✅
Use evidence to support claims.
Every conclusion should trace back to something observable.
